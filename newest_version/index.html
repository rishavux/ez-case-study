Project Overview

Problem

EZ Brow & Beauty never developed a digital presence that served its identity. The existing website saw low traffic and minimal conversion, failing to fulfill its basic purpose. Service offerings were inaccessible on digital platforms.

Solution

A complete brand and UX system — color palette, typography, voice, and information architecture — developed through market research and direct observation, then translated into a fully redesigned mobile experience.

Delivery

A fully designed mobile menu and cart-building experience. The system preserves and strengthens the existing brand identity, while making clear offerings, with pricing & service information in detail.

The Starting Point

EZ Brow & Beauty is my mom's business, and it has sustained my family for more than a decade. She did it as a single mother and an immigrant from Nepal. I watched my mom go to work every single day, building this business from the ground up. She has loyal clientele who have stayed with her for years, and that loyalty alone is a profound accomplishment. The business deserves to shine — both digitally and physically. The story behind it is rich, but it's always been implicit, never told outright. Design is the medium through which that story could finally be crafted and shared with the world. That realization mattered to me. I could contribute to her business not just by helping it grow, but as an act of reverence — a tribute to what she's already built. I genuinely want to bring her success into a new light.

Design Themes

Royal, Regal & Passionate

The color palette was inherited. Shades of red, gold, and silver were already present in our home, carried through objects my mom cherished for years. Growing up in Nepal, her sense of beauty was shaped by an idea of royalty. I wanted the design to reflect that same regal sensibility.

Precise & Penetrative

The typography needed to feel sharp, penetrative, and precise — qualities that come directly from my mother's Type A personality. She's bold in her speech, precise in her craft, and brave in her actions. Cinzel and Spectral were chosen to reflect her clean, confident, and bold presence.

Familiarity & Hospitality

The redesign needed to be made with existing clients in mind. We didn't want to disrupt what already worked. In practice, that meant preserving the same Vagaro booking flow they were familiar with, so returning felt like being welcomed back to something familiar.

Clarity & Conversion

Royalty gave the brand its color. Precision gave it its voice. Familiarity gave it trust. But, none of it mattered if a client wasn't clear about the services that we offered. Every screen was designed to enhance clarity and guide users toward booking a service appointment.

Key Design Decisions

1. Services, Reimagined.

The current Vagaro service menu lists every offering at the same visual weight, with no hierarchy and no sense of value. The redesign organizes services by category, paired with suitable images and icons for each one. Premium offerings like facials include a "Learn More" button that opens a dedicated product card with full service details, rendering context that previously did not exist.

2. Familiarity Maintained, Clarity Added.

The underlying booking flow remained unchanged to preserve familiarity for existing clients. New functionality was introduced through pop-up modals layered into that familiar structure — a product detail card explaining each premium service, a suggested add-ons modal appearing after the primary selection, and a secondary modal offering further detail on individual add-ons before a client commits to selecting them.

3. AI as an Assistant, not Designer.

AI generated raw material throughout this project — icon concepts, service photography, early color direction, and language to react to and rebuild. I edited, vectorized, rewrote, or discarded what didn't work. I also used it for execution, writing plugin code that built this project's entire color system in seconds. In every case, I stayed the editor, the decision-maker, and the only one responsible for what shipped.

Reflection

I don't think my mom has ever had the chance to see EZ Brow & Beauty as something she built, something with its own identity, something genuinely hers in the way a piece of art belongs to the person who made it. That is what this project is actually for. Not just a better booking flow. Not just a more legible service list. The goal underneath all of it is for my mother to look at what she's created and be proud of it.

I learned a great deal from this project. It is the first time I have built something from scratch, all the way through to something tangible and coherent. But the truth is that it is nowhere near being fully finished. I've come to understand that as long as a business is alive, the work never really stops, it only keeps refining.

My intention was to be detail-oriented throughout the project, making sure every decision was made with both the user and the stakeholder in mind. Even so, this project is far from complete. What exists today is the mobile experience, still living as a Figma prototype. I haven't yet designed for desktop or tablet, and I still have to get more technical and learn to turn all of this into real code. There's a long way to go, and that's humbling.

My intention from the beginning was bigger than what's built so far. I wanted to create a full design system and booking flow that could eventually replace Vagaro entirely for my mom's business. My brother, a senior software developer, is convinced we could build it ourselves and avoid the platform fees for good. That means designing and building a complete, private booking system from scratch. Right now, the experience stops just before someone selects a date, time, and pays. Resolving that is part of the original vision.

The scope of this project has been its biggest constraint. I've learned to work a few pixels at a time while still holding the larger vision in my head. Even with everything left to do, this is the project I've worked hardest on, and it's the one I'm proudest of.

What's Next?

Physical Renovation

User experience doesn't end at the screen. The moment a client walks through the door, the experience continues — and right now, the physical space doesn't reflect the same identity we built digitally. The goal is full cohesion: the same principles of royalty, precision, and warmth that guided the design system should be felt the moment someone steps inside. Beyond the client experience, my mom deserves to walk into a space that feels like hers — one that carries the same sense of empowerment the brand was built around. This is a long-term investment, and we're approaching it that way. She and I are actively building a strategy together to renovate the space thoughtfully, with the larger ambition of making EZ Brow & Beauty one of the most distinctly branded small businesses in San Francisco.

Herbal Product Line

This line isn't a product extension — it's an expression of where we come from. Our culture has always had its own medicine, its own beauty standards, and a deep body of knowledge about what the body needs and why. That wisdom is the foundation this product line is built on. As my mom pursues her bachelor's degree in Ayurveda, she has identified something the modern cosmetics industry largely ignores: that many widely used products are neither sustainable nor safe in the long run, and that the alternatives rooted in traditional plant-based formulation are not only more effective but more honest. Her intention is to look back — to the herbs, the rituals, and the compositions that have existed for centuries — and bring them forward in a form that is accessible, beautifully presented, and genuinely good for the people using them. The five products shown here are the beginning of that vision.

Custom Booking & Full Platform Experience

The mobile prototype is the proof of concept. What comes next is a fully custom booking system designed and developed from the ground up, replacing Vagaro entirely and eliminating the platform dependency that currently sits between this brand and its clients. My brother, a senior software developer, is already aligned on making this happen. Alongside that, the experience needs to scale — desktop and tablet designs that carry the same system, the same identity, and the same intentionality across every screen a client might use. The ambition from the beginning was never a prototype. It was a living, breathing brand platform that my mom owns completely — the design, the code, the booking flow, the product line, the physical space. All of it. This case study documents where that started.
